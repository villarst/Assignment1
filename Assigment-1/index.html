How I completed my website.

I started off first by playing around with some code from class. I really didn't understand the HTML syntax well so I begin to experiment with different tags and elements. I also looked at the slides provided from class but they didn't really help. Most of the stuff I put in this document I came up with was from the W3 website. It had a lot of code that helped with what I was doing.

To start off how I coded this website. I do have to say a bulk of the time was used to create the grid layout. My gosh that was hard. It took me two days to figure it out, but I essentially just measured the screen with a ruler and figured out from there how many rows and columns I would need and how long they would span. It turned out well, so I am happy I got that part somewhat right.

I also could not figure out how to line up the top with my name, address, and the icon. That took a couple of hours but I couldn't get my name to be lined up correctly with the phone number. It got really annoying after almost two hours of trying and failing miserably, but I tried my best to align them.

Everything else was pretty straight forward. Padding had to be checked in multiple grid areas but I liked the way it turned out. This definetly will prepare me for my capstone project since it is mostly webbased.

I do have to mention that about 3/4's through I stopped giving tags ID's and started to associate them with the class since it was easier to do. For instance, box8 had 'a' and an 'img'. Typing .box8 a and .box8 img instead of what I had mention in CSS made things a lot cleaner in my opinion. Glad I picked that up and will more than likely use that for future projects.

When I was 17 I was diagnosed with a rare type of Brain Cancer called a Pilomyxoid Astrocytoma. It was a type of cancer that is inoperable and something that I more than likely have to live with forever until a cure comes around. This cancer is bad but not at the same time. Cancers are graded on a scale of I-IV with IV (4) being the worst with a grim diagnosis. My cancer is considered to be a Grade II type of tumor so the outcome is likely in my favor.

However this does not mean that I am safe from it impacting me in a negative way. This cancer can cause a plethora of issues include, permanent vision loss, strabismus (Lazy Eye), and other issues depending on where it is located in the brain. All of which I currently have.

It sucks having to live with this disease and all I can do is hope that there will be a cure one day. People have been cured by this type of cancer using Chemotherapy but it is rare and more research is needed for a viable and sustainable cure to make this cancer go away for ever.
